1. A consumer problem worth solving
Most football fans already watch with a phone in hand. Yet second screens are usually raw scoreboards, noisy social feeds, or betting products. PulseProof is different. It explains why a moment matters, protects fans who missed the ending, and lets them preserve a verifiable memory on Solana. There are no wagers or financial rewards.

2. The deployed consumer product
This is the public Railway deployment. The multi-match center shows real TxLINE devnet coverage beside clearly labelled, source-linked historical replays. It never calls a waiting fixture live. The command center adds the confirmed road to the final, smart alerts, followed-team context, and an explicit T B D whenever a winner is not yet known.

3. The complete matchday loop
PulseProof covers the whole matchday. Before kick-off, fans get source-linked fixtures, local time, reminders, and calendar export. During play, one server-sent event connection updates up to eight covered matches. Afterward, a ninety-second Catch-up explains only the signal moments, and Proof of Watch turns a chosen moment into a non-transferable fan memory.

4. Catch-up without spoilers
For a fan who missed the match, Catch-up is progressive. Spoiler Shield hides the final score, scorers, cards, timeline, and final momentum. Event one cannot leak event seven. This Portugal versus Spain replay is cross-checked against a published report and is labelled as replay data. Mikel Merino's winner, stoppage time, assist, and cards are preserved exactly.

5. TxLINE integration and accuracy
Accuracy is a product feature. Credentials remain server-side while PulseProof uses TxLINE fixture snapshots, score snapshots, historical scores, stat validation, and the canonical score stream. The public endpoint has returned ready, pulse, and a real heartbeat. Sparse devnet fixtures never receive invented competitions or start times. Eliminated teams are never placed into future fixtures.

6. Offline recap and retention
Retention also means working on weak networks. Fans may save up to eight finished recaps. The pack contains only transformed, on-pitch consumer moments. Technical actions and reward fields are removed. The service worker caches the application shell, but never caches an API or S S E response. Offline streaming, attestations, and claims all fail closed until reconnection.

7. Proof of Watch on Solana
When a fan chooses a moment, the server re-checks its source and signs a short-lived canonical message. The message binds the wallet, fixture, moment hash, evidence digest, points, badge, and expiry. Solana's Ed two five five one nine precompile verifies that signature, then the PulseProof program creates a deterministic Moment Receipt and updates the non-transferable Fan Pass.

9. Smart-contract safety
The contract fails closed. The Ed two five five one nine instruction must immediately precede the claim. The configured attestor is pinned. Proofs expire within five minutes, far-future expiries are rejected, and points and badges are clamped. Receipt P D A uniqueness blocks duplicates. No token is minted, no reward is transferable, and no API secret reaches the browser.
